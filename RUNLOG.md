\# RUNLOG



\## Objective



Build a causal end-of-turn (EOT) detector that predicts whether a pause is a true end of turn (`eot`) or a temporary hesitation (`hold`).



Primary metric: minimize mean response delay while keeping interrupted turns at or below 5%.



All features are computed strictly from audio available before `pause\_start`. No future audio is used.



\---



\## Baseline



\### Constant / Provided Baseline



English result:



\- AUC: 0.514

\- Mean response delay: 1600 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 0%



This baseline was safe but responded too slowly.



\---



\## Starter Model



Used the provided starter features:



\- Energy immediately before pause

\- Final pitch

\- Available speech-context duration



Model:



\- Logistic Regression

\- Balanced class weights



English result:



\- AUC: 0.599

\- Mean response delay: 1190 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



This demonstrated that simple prosodic information contains useful EOT signal.



\---



\## Experiment 1 - Richer Prosodic Features



Added:



\- Final energy

\- Mean energy

\- Energy drop

\- Final pitch

\- Mean pitch

\- Pitch change

\- Pitch standard deviation

\- Voiced fraction



English result:



\- AUC: 0.605

\- Mean response delay: 1315 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



Hindi in-sample result:



\- AUC: 0.664

\- Mean response delay: 826 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 4%



Observation:



The richer feature set appeared substantially stronger on Hindi. However, the 826 ms result was obtained after fitting and predicting on the same dataset, so it was treated as optimistic and not used as the primary validation estimate.



\---



\## Experiment 2 - Expanded Causal Prosody



Added additional causal features:



\### Energy



\- Final energy

\- Mean energy

\- Energy variability

\- Relative energy drop

\- Recent energy slope



\### Pitch



\- Final pitch

\- Mean pitch

\- Pitch variability

\- Recent pitch slope

\- Relative final pitch



\### Voicing



\- Overall voiced fraction

\- Final voiced fraction

\- Final voiced run



\### Turn Context



\- Elapsed time at pause

\- Pause index

\- Available context duration



All audio-derived features use only samples before `pause\_start`.



Two classifiers were compared:



1\. Logistic Regression with feature standardization

2\. Histogram Gradient Boosting



A held-out-turn split showed:



\- Logistic Regression accuracy: 0.569

\- Gradient Boosting accuracy: 0.621



This motivated more rigorous out-of-fold evaluation.



\---



\## Experiment 3 - Grouped Out-of-Fold Validation



To avoid optimistic evaluation, 5-fold GroupKFold validation was introduced.



All pauses belonging to the same turn were kept within the same fold.



Therefore, every OOF prediction was generated by a model that had never trained on that turn.



\### Hindi - Gradient Boosting



\- Mean OOF accuracy: 0.593

\- AUC: 0.617

\- Mean response delay: 865 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



\### Hindi - Logistic Regression



\- Mean OOF accuracy: 0.625

\- AUC: 0.654

\- Mean response delay: 850 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



Logistic Regression was selected because it produced both higher AUC and lower response delay.



\### English - Logistic Regression



\- Mean OOF accuracy: 0.565

\- AUC: 0.596

\- Mean response delay: 1301 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



The large language-dependent performance difference motivated language-specialized models.



\---



\## Ensemble Experiment



A weighted ensemble was tested using:



\- 75% Logistic Regression probability

\- 25% Gradient Boosting probability



Hindi OOF result:



\- AUC: 0.655

\- Mean response delay: 850 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



The ensemble did not improve the primary delay metric and was rejected.



\---



\## Pause Position Adjustment



A heuristic probability adjustment increased EOT probability for later pause indices.



Hindi OOF result:



\- AUC: 0.656

\- Mean response delay: 850 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



This also failed to improve the primary metric and was rejected.



\---



\## Final Architecture



The final system uses three saved Logistic Regression pipelines:



1\. Hindi specialist

2\. English specialist

3\. Global fallback



Each pipeline includes:



\- StandardScaler

\- LogisticRegression with balanced class weights



Inference routing:



\- Hindi data -> Hindi specialist

\- English data -> English specialist

\- Unknown language -> Global fallback



Language detection first checks the dataset folder name and then known turn/audio identifier prefixes. If neither provides a reliable signal, the global model is used.



The inference script loads previously saved models and does not refit on unseen evaluation data.

\## Final Validation



Honest grouped OOF validation:



Hindi:



\- AUC: 0.654

\- Mean response delay: 850 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



English:



\- AUC: 0.596

\- Mean response delay: 1301 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



Final Hindi specialist end-to-end hygeine check:



\- AUC: 0.725

\- Mean response delay: 850 ms

\- Interrupted turns: 5%



The final sanity-check score is in-sample and is reported only to verify the complete saved-model inference pipeline. The grouped OOF results are the primary estimate of generalization performance.



\---



\## Key Learnings



1\. EOT detection contains useful signal in prosody even without lexical information.

2\. Energy and pitch trajectories are more informative than isolated endpoint values.

3\. Evaluation must be grouped by turn to prevent leakage.

4\. Higher classification accuracy or AUC does not necessarily translate into lower response delay under the strict 5% interruption constraint.

5\. Hindi and English showed materially different performance, motivating specialist models with a global fallback.

6\. Simple Logistic Regression generalized better than the tested nonlinear boosting model on the primary Hindi evaluation.
